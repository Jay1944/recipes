Dosa

South India is a vibrant, distinct region spanning the peninsula below the Vindhya range, bounded by the Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and Indian Ocean. It comprises five states—Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Kerala, Tamil Nadu, and Telangana—characterized by tropical climates, rich Dravidian culture, ancient temple architecture, lush landscapes, and distinct, rice-based, coconut-infused cuisine

A dosa (in Indian English) or thosai (in Malaysian English and Singapore English)[1] is a thin, savoury batter bread in South Indian cuisine made from a fermented batter of ground black gram and rice. Dosas are served hot, often with chutney and sambar.

Ingredients

  • Rice: 3 cups Sona Masuri or Idli rice
  • Urad Dal: 1 cup whole skinned black gram (urad dal)
  • Fenugreek Seeds: 1 tsp (methi seeds)
  • Poha (Flattened Rice): 1/4 cup (optional, for softness)
  • Salt: To taste (approx. 1 tsp rock salt)
  • Oil: For cooking (ghee or vegetable oil)

Recipe

  1. Soak (4-6 hours) Rinse the rice and dal thoroughly until the water is clear. Soak the rice and fenugreek seeds in one bowl, and the urad dal and poha in another bowl for at least 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind to Batter (5-10 minutes) Drain the soaked dal and grind to a very smooth, fluffy paste with a little water. Drain the rice and grind to a slightly coarse paste. Combine the rice and dal batters in a large pot. Add salt and mix thoroughly, preferably with your hands, to introduce bacteria for fermentation.
  3. Ferment (8-12 hours) Cover the container and let it ferment in a warm place for 8–12 hours, or until the batter has doubled in size and looks light and bubbly. Tip: If the weather is cold, keep it inside an oven with the light on.
  4. Make the Dosa Gently stir the fermented batter. If it is too thick, add a little water. Heat a non-stick or cast-iron tawa/griddle on medium heat. Once hot, wipe it with an oiled cloth or paper towel to ensure the batter spreads easily. Pour a ladle of batter in the center and spread it in a circular motion to make a thin, circular, thin pancake. Spread a teaspoon of oil around the edges and on top. Cook until the bottom is golden brown and the edges lift, then fold and serve hot.
