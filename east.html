Rasgulla

East India, a region of India including West Bengal, Bihar, Jharkhand, Odisha, and sometimes Chhattisgarh, is a culturally rich area known for the Ganges delta, rice-based cuisine, and Bengali sweets.

Rasgulla (literally "syrup-filled ball") is a syrupy dessert popular in the eastern part of South Asia. It is made from ball-shaped dumplings of chhena dough cooked in light sugar syrup until the syrup permeates the dumplings.

Ingredients

  • Milk: 1 liter full-fat/whole cow milk
  • Acid: 2-3 tablespoons lemon juice or white vinegar
  • Binder: 1 teaspoon all-purpose flour (maida) or semolina (suji) - Optional, helps in binding
  • Syrup: 1.5 cups white sugar, 4 cups water, 3 cardamom pods,tsp rose water (optional)

Recipe

  1. Prepare Chenna: Boil 1 liter milk in a pot, turn off heat, and add lemon juice gradually while stirring until the whey separates.
  2. Strain and Rinse: Strain the chenna using a muslin cloth and rinse it thoroughly with cold water to remove the lemon taste.
  3. Hang: Squeeze out excess water and hang the cloth for 1-2 hours to remove all water.
  4. Knead: Transfer the chenna to a plate and knead for 7-10 minutes until smooth, non-sticky, and soft to touch.
  5. Shape: Make small, crack-free balls from the kneaded dough.
  6. Make Syrup & Cook: In a large pot or pressure cooker, mix 1.5 cups sugar and 4 cups water, and bring to a boil. Add the balls to the boiling syrup, cover, and cook for 15-20 minutes on high to medium heat.
  7. Cool: Let the rasgullas cool completely in the syrup before serving.
